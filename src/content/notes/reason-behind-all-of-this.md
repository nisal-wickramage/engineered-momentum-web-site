# Reason Behind All of This

Software engineering has no shortage of ideas.

Over the past few decades we've learned about feedback from Extreme Programming, continuous improvement from Lean, safe releases through Continuous Delivery, maintainable code through Refactoring and A Philosophy of Software Design, scalable architectures through Domain-Driven Design and Building Evolutionary Architectures, organizational design through Team Topologies, and operational excellence through DevOps and SRE.

These ideas have transformed how we build software.

Yet they're often discussed in isolation.

In reality, organizations rarely ask, "Should we adopt Domain-Driven Design?" or "Do we need Continuous Delivery?" They face challenges like delivering changes faster, scaling engineering teams, expanding into new markets, supporting enterprise customers, adopting AI, or doing more with fewer resources.

This website is my attempt to connect those islands.

Not by inventing another methodology, but by studying how proven engineering principles help organizations solve real business challenges.

Every article explores one question through four lenses:

- Optimize for Change — How do we reduce the cost and risk of change?
- Scale Engineering — How do teams and systems grow without creating complexity?
- Expand the Business — How does engineering enable new products, customers, markets, and regulations?
- Increase Engineering Leverage — How do AI, automation, and platforms amplify engineering capability?

Think of these as notes for my future self.

As I read classic books, study modern engineering practices, build software, and learn from organizations that do this well, I'll capture the ideas that stand the test of time and connect them to the challenges engineering leaders face today.

If those notes help me and you make better engineering decisions, then this website has served its purpose.
